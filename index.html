Mini Quadcopter




The Mini Quadcopter was one of my favourite hardware projects to have worked on. The frame is 3D printed, with the whole device taking on an ultralight appeal weighing only 73g in total. I had a lot of fun building this project and would definitely consider building a larger prototype in the future.

Othello/Reversi AI




In grade 12, I made a Player vs Othello AI game that was run on a Raspberry Pi. This was one of my favourite projects by far. I learned from first hand experience how digital monitors worked and even a few things about human psychology. In total, the project had 128 Led lights and 32 wires forming an 8x8 grid.

Self-balancing Robot




Designed a self-balancing robot with Arduino Nano and MPU6050. It helped me experiment and learn in-depth about the PID Controller.

Programmed using C++ and designed all relevant circuitry.

Quadruped Robot




I've always found the little Hexbugs sold in toystores to be fascinating and wanted to figure out how they worked. Through hands on experimentation, I was able to learn a lot about these creepers; the way they move about is called the "Creep Gait", which involves the retraction of the body towards the center of mass every time a leg is lifted. While it was a daunting task at first, I overcame various challenges and learned much on the art of power management and improved my soldering and programming skills.

Ping Pong Turret




For my culminating task in grade 12 tech engineering class, I made a ping pong ball turret that was wirelessly controlled. The command is sent via ssh from a thrid party app on my mobile device, then the Raspberry Pi interprets and executes the command. The stepping motor controls the turrets rotations and the servo releases the ball from the collector.
